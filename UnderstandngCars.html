How Engines Work

Gas (Otto)

  1. INTAKE: Intake valve opens, Feul air drawn to cylinder, piston moves down
  2. Compression: Piston Reaches bottom, intake valve close, piston moves up compressing gas
  3. Combustion/Power: Compress gas + spak plug creates explosion. Explosion moves piston down.
  4. Exhaust: Once gas has burned, exhaust valve opens, Burnt gas goes out
  5. Exhaust valve closes, intake valve opens, process repeats.

    Video on "How Engines Work".

Diesel

  1. INTAKE: Intake valve opens, air drawn to cylinder, piston moves down
  2. Compression: Piston Reaches bottom, intake valve close, piston moves up compressing air heating it up
  3. Combustion/Power: Once air is compressed and heated, feul is injected.
  4. Once feul comes in contact with hot compressed air, it ignites and explode.(no spark plug needed in the process)
  5. Explosion moves piston down.
  6. Exhaust: Once gas has burned, exhaust valve opens, Burnt gas goes out
  7. Exhaust valve closes, intake valve opens, process repeats.
